Expert judgment is defined as judgment provided based upon expertise in an application area, Knowledge Area, 
discipline, industry, etc., as appropriate for the activity being performed

## DEVELOP PROJECT CHARTER
For this process, expertise should be considered from individuals or groups with specialized knowledge of or training in the following topics:
1. Organizational strategy
2. Benefits management
3. Technical knowledge of the industry and focus area of the project
4. Duration and budget estimation
5. Risk identification

## DEVELOP PROJECT MANAGEMENT PLAN
Expertise should be considered from individuals or groups with specialized knowledge of or training in the following topics:
1. Tailoring the project management process to meet the project needs, including the dependencies and interactions among those processes and the essential inputs and outputs
2. Developing additional components of the project management plan if needed
3. Determining the tools and techniques to be used for accomplishing those processes
4. Developing technical and management details to be included in the project management plan
5. Determining resources and skill levels needed to perform project work
6. Defining the level of configuration management to apply on the project
7. Determining which project documents will be subject to the formal change control process
8. Prioritizing the work on the project to ensure the project resources are allocated to the appropriate work at the appropriate time.

## PLAN RESOURCE MANAGEMENT
Expertise should be considered from individuals or groups with specialized knowledge or training in the following topics:
1. Negotiating for the best resources within the organization
2. Talent management and personnel development
3. Determining the preliminary effort level needed to meet project objectives
4. Determining reporting requirements based on the organizational culture
5. Estimating lead times required for acquisition, based on lessons learned and market conditions
6. Identifying risks associated with resource acquisition, retention, and release plans
7. Complying with applicable government and union regulations
8. Managing sellers and the logistics effort to ensure materials and supplies are available when needed.
